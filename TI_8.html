Hull Moving Average (HMA)

A powerful trend-following indicator designed to reduce lag while maintaining smooth price action. The Hull Moving Average helps traders identify trends earlier than traditional moving averages, making it popular among swing traders, intraday traders, and investors.

What is the Hull Moving Average (HMA)?

The Hull Moving Average (HMA), developed by Alan Hull in 2005, is an advanced moving average designed to solve two common problems associated with traditional moving averages:

  • Lagging behind price movements.
  • Producing excessive noise when made more responsive.

HMA combines weighted moving averages and mathematical smoothing techniques to provide a smoother and faster-reacting trend indicator. It responds quickly to market changes while filtering out minor fluctuations.

How the HMA is Calculated

The Hull Moving Average uses Weighted Moving Averages (WMA) rather than Simple Moving Averages (SMA).

Step 1:
WMA(n/2) × 2 − WMA(n)
Step 2:
Calculate the Weighted Moving Average of the result using √n period.
Final Formula:
HMA = WMA [ 2 × WMA(n/2) − WMA(n), √n ]

This unique calculation significantly reduces lag while preserving trend accuracy.

Why Traders Use HMA

Lower Lag

HMA reacts faster to price changes than SMA and EMA, allowing traders to identify trend reversals sooner.

Smooth Trend Detection

Despite being responsive, HMA remains smooth and reduces market noise.

Clear Signals

Traders can easily identify bullish and bearish trends using slope changes.

Versatile Usage

Suitable for stocks, forex, commodities, crypto, and indices.

Trading Signals from HMA

1. Trend Direction

  • Upward HMA = Bullish trend.
  • Downward HMA = Bearish trend.
  • Flat HMA = Consolidation or sideways market.

2. Price Crossing HMA

  • Price moves above HMA → Potential buy signal.
  • Price moves below HMA → Potential sell signal.

3. HMA Turning Points

Many traders focus on the turning points of the HMA rather than crossovers. A change in slope often indicates an early shift in momentum.

4. Multiple HMA Strategy

Combining a short-term and long-term HMA can generate crossover signals similar to traditional moving-average systems.

Recommended HMA Period Settings

Intraday Trading 20 – 34 Trend identification
Swing Trading 50 – 100 Medium-term trends
Long-Term Investing 150 – 200 Major trend analysis

Advantages of HMA

  • Reduces lag significantly.
  • Provides smoother trend visualization.
  • Generates earlier entry opportunities.
  • Works well across multiple markets.
  • Easy to combine with RSI, MACD, and price action strategies.

Limitations of HMA

  • Can produce false signals in highly volatile markets.
  • Not ideal during prolonged sideways movement.
  • Should not be used as a standalone indicator.
  • Requires confirmation from volume or momentum indicators.

Professional Trading Tip

Many professional traders combine the Hull Moving Average with RSI and support/resistance analysis. Instead of relying solely on crossovers, they focus on HMA slope changes, trend direction, and confirmation from market structure to improve trade accuracy.
