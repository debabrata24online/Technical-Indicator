Bollinger Bands

A powerful volatility-based technical analysis indicator used in trading markets

Overview

What are Bollinger Bands?

Bollinger Bands are a technical analysis tool created by John Bollinger. They are used to measure market volatility and identify potential overbought or oversold conditions.

They consist of three lines plotted on a price chart:

  • Middle Band: Simple Moving Average (SMA), usually 20 periods
  • Upper Band: SMA + 2 standard deviations
  • Lower Band: SMA - 2 standard deviations

How It Works

The bands expand and contract based on market volatility:

  • High volatility: Bands widen
  • Low volatility: Bands contract

Price movements near the upper band may indicate overbought conditions, while near the lower band may indicate oversold conditions.

Trading Signals

  • 📈 Breakout: Price moving outside bands can signal strong momentum
  • 🔄 Reversal: Price returning inside bands may indicate reversal
  • ⚠️ Squeeze: Narrow bands often precede big price moves

Bollinger Squeeze

A Bollinger Squeeze occurs when the bands contract tightly, indicating very low volatility. This often precedes a strong breakout in either direction.

Important Limitations

  • Not a standalone indicator — should be combined with volume or momentum indicators
  • False signals may occur in sideways markets
  • Works best in trending or volatile markets

Practical Example

If a stock is trading in a strong uptrend and repeatedly touches the upper band, it does not always mean it will reverse — it may indicate strong momentum instead.

Traders often combine Bollinger Bands with RSI or MACD for confirmation.
