Ichimoku Cloud Indicator

A complete guide to understanding one of the most powerful trading indicators

Overview

What is Ichimoku Cloud?

The Ichimoku Cloud (Ichimoku Kinko Hyo) is a comprehensive technical analysis indicator used in trading to identify trend direction, support, resistance, and momentum all at once. It was developed by Japanese journalist Goichi Hosoda before World War II.

Core Idea

Why is it powerful?

Unlike many indicators that show only one aspect of the market, Ichimoku provides a complete picture:

  • Trend direction (bullish or bearish)
  • Momentum strength
  • Support & resistance levels
  • Future price projections
Components

Five Key Lines of Ichimoku

Tenkan-sen (Conversion Line)

Short-term trend indicator. Calculated using the highest high and lowest low over 9 periods.

Kijun-sen (Base Line)

Medium-term trend indicator using 26 periods. Acts as support/resistance.

Senkou Span A

First leading line forming the cloud boundary, projected 26 periods ahead.

Senkou Span B

Second cloud boundary based on 52-period range, also projected forward.

Chikou Span

Lagging line representing current price plotted 26 periods back.

Cloud (Kumo)

Understanding the Ichimoku Cloud

The area between Senkou Span A and B is called the Kumo (cloud). It acts as dynamic support and resistance.

  • Bullish Cloud: Span A above Span B
  • Bearish Cloud: Span B above Span A
  • Thicker cloud = stronger support/resistance
Trading Signals

How Traders Use It

  • Price above cloud → bullish trend
  • Price below cloud → bearish trend
  • Price inside cloud → sideways market
  • Tenkan cross Kijun → buy/sell signals
Summary

Final Insight

The Ichimoku Cloud is a complete trading system rather than just an indicator. It helps traders quickly understand market structure, trend strength, and potential future price direction.
