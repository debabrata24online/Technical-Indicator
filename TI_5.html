Coppock Curve Indicator

A long-term momentum indicator designed to identify major buying opportunities in financial markets by measuring market momentum and detecting long-term trend reversals.

What is the Coppock Curve?

The Coppock Curve is a momentum-based technical indicator developed by economist and market analyst Edwin Sedgwick Coppock in 1962. It was originally designed to identify long-term buying opportunities in stock market indexes.

Coppock believed that market declines resemble periods of grief, and after a significant decline investors require time to recover emotionally before returning to the market. Based on this concept, he created an indicator capable of identifying the end of bear markets and the beginning of new bullish trends.

"The Coppock Curve is primarily used as a long-term buy signal indicator rather than a short-term trading tool."

How the Coppock Curve is Calculated

The indicator combines two Rate of Change (ROC) calculations and smooths them using a Weighted Moving Average (WMA).

Coppock Curve = WMA( ROC(14) + ROC(11), 10 )

Calculation Steps

  1. Calculate the 14-month Rate of Change (ROC).
  2. Calculate the 11-month Rate of Change (ROC).
  3. Add both ROC values together.
  4. Apply a 10-period Weighted Moving Average.
  5. The resulting value forms the Coppock Curve.

Key Components

Rate of Change (ROC)

Measures the percentage change in price compared to a previous period, indicating market momentum strength.

Weighted Moving Average

Smooths price fluctuations and gives greater weight to recent market data.

Momentum Analysis

Helps traders determine whether bullish momentum is increasing or decreasing over time.

How to Interpret the Coppock Curve

Signal Meaning
Curve Turns Up from Below Zero Strong Long-Term Buy Signal
Curve Rising Above Zero Bullish Momentum Strengthening
Curve Falling Momentum Weakening
Curve Below Zero Market Remains Weak

Trading Signals

1. Buy Signal

The most important signal occurs when the Coppock Curve changes direction and turns upward after a prolonged decline below the zero line. This often suggests the beginning of a new long-term bullish trend.

2. Trend Confirmation

A steadily rising Coppock Curve indicates strengthening momentum and supports an existing uptrend.

3. Divergence Analysis

If prices make new lows while the Coppock Curve makes higher lows, a bullish divergence may be forming, indicating a potential reversal.

Advantages

  • Excellent for long-term trend identification.
  • Reduces market noise.
  • Simple interpretation.
  • Useful for investors and position traders.
  • Effective in identifying major market bottoms.

Limitations

  • Lagging indicator by nature.
  • Not ideal for intraday trading.
  • Can miss early portions of trends.
  • Less effective in sideways markets.
  • Should not be used alone.

Best Practices

Combine with Trend Analysis

Use support/resistance levels and trendlines to confirm signals.

Use with Volume

Increasing volume can strengthen the reliability of a Coppock Curve buy signal.

Long-Term Focus

Works best on weekly and monthly charts rather than short-term timeframes.

Risk Management

Always use proper position sizing and stop-loss strategies.

Conclusion

The Coppock Curve is a powerful long-term momentum indicator primarily used to identify major market bottoms and emerging bullish trends. While it is not suitable for short-term trading, it remains highly respected among investors seeking long-term trend confirmation.

For the best results, combine the Coppock Curve with trend analysis, volume studies, and other technical indicators rather than relying on it as a standalone system.
