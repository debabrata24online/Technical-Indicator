Williams Alligator Indicator

Learn how the Alligator Indicator helps traders identify trends, avoid sideways markets, detect breakouts, and improve trading decisions using Bill Williams' unique market psychology approach.

What is the Williams Alligator Indicator?

The Williams Alligator Indicator is a trend-following technical indicator developed by legendary trader Bill Williams. It helps traders determine whether a market is trending or moving sideways.


The indicator uses three smoothed moving averages that represent different parts of an alligator:


  • Jaw – Long-term trend line
  • Teeth – Medium-term trend line
  • Lips – Short-term trend line

Williams compared markets to an alligator. When the alligator is sleeping, markets move sideways. When it wakes up and opens its mouth, strong trends often emerge.

The Three Components

🐊 Jaw (Blue Line)

13-period Smoothed Moving Average shifted 8 bars forward.


Represents the long-term market balance and trend direction.

🐊 Teeth (Red Line)

8-period Smoothed Moving Average shifted 5 bars forward.


Represents the medium-term market structure.

🐊 Lips (Green Line)

5-period Smoothed Moving Average shifted 3 bars forward.


Reacts fastest to price movement and identifies new trends.

Indicator Formula

The Alligator Indicator uses Smoothed Moving Averages (SMMA):

Jaw = 13-period SMMA shifted 8 bars forward

Teeth = 8-period SMMA shifted 5 bars forward

Lips = 5-period SMMA shifted 3 bars forward

The forward shifting creates a visual representation of market momentum and trend evolution.

The Four Alligator Market Phases

😴 Sleeping Alligator

The three lines are intertwined and moving close together. This indicates a ranging or sideways market with no clear trend.

👀 Awakening Alligator

The lines begin separating. A new trend may be starting and traders begin watching for breakout opportunities.

🍽 Hungry Alligator

The lines are clearly separated and moving in the same direction. This is often the strongest trending phase.

😌 Satisfied Alligator

The lines begin converging again, indicating weakening momentum and a possible trend ending.

Trading Signals

Bullish Signal


  • Lips cross above Teeth
  • Teeth cross above Jaw
  • Price remains above all lines
  • Lines spread apart

This suggests a developing uptrend.

Bearish Signal


  • Lips cross below Teeth
  • Teeth cross below Jaw
  • Price remains below all lines
  • Lines spread apart downward

This suggests a developing downtrend.

Example Interpretation

Condition Meaning
Lines intertwined Market consolidation
Lips cross upward Potential bullish trend
Lines widely separated Strong trend underway
Lines converging Trend weakening

Combining with Williams Fractals

Bill Williams designed Fractals and the Alligator Indicator to work together.


  • Use the Alligator to determine trend direction.
  • Use Fractals to identify breakout levels.
  • Take long trades above bullish fractal breakouts during uptrends.
  • Take short trades below bearish fractal breakouts during downtrends.

This combination remains one of the most popular Williams trading systems.

Advantages & Limitations

Advantages


  • Excellent trend identification.
  • Filters out sideways markets.
  • Easy visual interpretation.
  • Works on multiple timeframes.
  • Combines well with Fractals and RSI.

Limitations


  • Lagging by nature.
  • False signals in choppy markets.
  • Late entries after major moves.
  • Requires confirmation tools.
  • Less effective during low volatility periods.
