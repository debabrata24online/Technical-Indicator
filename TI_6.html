What is the Awesome Oscillator?

Understanding one of the most popular momentum indicators in technical analysis.

The Awesome Oscillator (AO) is a momentum-based technical indicator created by renowned trader Bill Williams. It measures market momentum by comparing a short-term moving average to a longer-term moving average.


AO helps traders determine whether bullish or bearish forces are currently dominating the market. It is displayed as a histogram oscillating around a zero line, making trend analysis visually intuitive.

Calculation Formula

How the Awesome Oscillator is calculated.

AO = SMA(5) of Median Price − SMA(34) of Median Price

Median Price = (High + Low) / 2

5-Period SMA

Represents short-term market momentum and reacts quickly to price changes.

34-Period SMA

Represents longer-term momentum and smooths out short-term fluctuations.

Histogram

The difference between these averages is plotted as green and red histogram bars around the zero line.

How to Interpret AO

Understanding bullish and bearish momentum.

Above Zero

Indicates bullish momentum. Buyers are gaining strength and the uptrend may continue.

Below Zero

Indicates bearish momentum. Sellers dominate and downward pressure may increase.

Crossing Zero Line

A transition between bullish and bearish momentum. Traders often view this as a potential trend signal.

Major Trading Signals

The three most popular AO trading setups.

1. Zero Line Cross

When AO crosses from below zero to above zero, it suggests bullish momentum. A move from above to below zero suggests bearish momentum.

2. Twin Peaks Pattern

A bullish twin peak occurs below the zero line when the second trough is higher than the first. A bearish twin peak occurs above the zero line.

3. Saucer Setup

A bullish saucer consists of two consecutive red bars followed by a green bar above zero. The bearish version occurs below zero.

Advantages & Limitations

Every indicator has strengths and weaknesses.

Advantages Limitations
Easy to read histogram structure Can generate false signals in sideways markets
Excellent for momentum analysis Not ideal as a standalone indicator
Useful for trend confirmation Lagging because it uses moving averages
Works across multiple timeframes Requires risk management and confirmation tools

Practical Trading Strategy

Combining AO with trend analysis.

Step 1

Identify the overall market trend using moving averages or price structure.

Step 2

Wait for AO to confirm momentum through a zero-line cross or saucer signal.

Step 3

Enter with trend confirmation and use stop-loss protection.

Step 4

Exit when momentum weakens or AO generates an opposite signal.

Final Thoughts

The Awesome Oscillator is one of the most effective momentum indicators for identifying trend strength and potential reversals. While it should not be used alone, combining it with support & resistance, moving averages, price action, and volume analysis can significantly improve trading decisions.
